Story Behind Hangul


1.

Hangul, the Korean alphabet was a project promoted by Sejong the Great, the fourth king of the Joseon Dynasty.


2.

The Korean alphabet was originally called Hunminjeong'eum. This means that it was designed for commoners would be able to read and write.


3.

King Sejong decided that the Korean people needed a new alphabet because the Korean language was fundamentally different from Chinese.


4.

A Korean syllable is divided into three parts: initial consonant, peak vowel, and final consonant.


5.

The shape of consonants were designed after the shapes the mouth while the vowels were designed after the principles of yin and yang.
