Form & Structure

1.

There are 14 consonants in the Korean alphabet. The five basic Hangeul consonants are: ㄱ, ㄴ, ㅁ, ㅅ, and ㅇ. Other letters are made by adding an extra stroke or stacking onto these basic five letters.

2.

Some consonants can be doubled. They make stronger sounds.

3.

The shapes of the vowels are based on three elements: man (a vertical line), earth (a horizontal line) and heaven (a dot). In modern Hangeul the heavenly dot has mutated into a short line.

4.

The consonants and vowels are stacked into little blocks for each syllable
